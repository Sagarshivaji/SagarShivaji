Stripe · Accessibility Overhaul

RoleAccessibility Lead
IndustryFintech
Timeline5 months
Reading time5 min
Overview Problem Execution Results

Overview

Updating Stripe's core dashboard templates to meet WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility guidelines.

Led research and frontend updates to rebuild focus states, screen reader guides, and color contrast models across complex transactional tables.

100%

WCAG 2.2 AA Compliance

Problem

Stripe's dashboard is data-dense. The payment charts, filter dropdowns, and transactional grids lacked proper ARIA attributes, keyboard-only tab paths, and high color-contrast options.

Many merchants who relied on screen-readers or keyboard-only navigation systems struggled to search transactions, export analytics, or resolve customer refund requests.

Execution Details

We refactored the design system's primitive components to build in accessibility constraints by default:

  • Interactive Charts: Built fallback tabular views readable by screen readers for complex line and bar graphs.
  • Keyboard Tab Rings: Designed high-contrast focus rings that remain visible and follow logical tab flows across dense grids.
  • Dynamic Contrast Styles: Implemented color token checks that automatically adjust text colors to maintain a 4.5:1 ratio against card backgrounds.

Results

100%

Compliance on critical merchant workflows

+30%

Access scores reported by enterprise accounts
